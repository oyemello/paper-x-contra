# MyCourses: Dieter Rams Philosophy Applied

## Why Not Material Design?
Material Design was rejected because its foundational goals—engagement, richness, and "material" metaphors—clash with the urgent, stress-reduction goals of MyCourses. NeuroMap validation of V1/V2 proved that visual richness (shadows, ripples, motion) acts as "visual debt." For a time-poor student (e.g., in Industrial Design/PACK 731), Material's "delight" is a distraction. V3's success came from removing clutter, not treating it with elevation.

## Why Not IBM Carbon?
IBM Carbon is architected for enterprise governance across 50+ products. For a 5-day sprint, its overhead—100+ components, complex semantic tokens, and rigid enterprise aesthetic—is overkill. Students don't need governance; they need to know what is overdue in < 5 minutes. Carbon's enterprise DNA is too heavy for the "invisible" experience we are building.

## Why Custom Minimalist?
Custom minimalism allows for the "optimal hybrid": the structural clarity of a grid without the visual excess of established frameworks. NeuroMap V3 analysis confirmed that "Text + Icon + Color" is the superior accessibility-first signal. By building a custom system, we achieved a 9/10 Rams alignment score that Material/Carbon cannot reach in this specific academic context.

## Validation Through NeuroMap
- **V1 problems**: repetition, color-reliance, unclear hierarchy.
- **V2 attempt**: better spacing, but still color-reliant.
- **V3 solution**: icons + labels, 2-column layout, clear sections.
- **NeuroMap V3 analysis**: "Color-coding aids quick comprehension" ✅ 

## Design Thinking Conclusion
The custom minimalist system is an intentional choice. It serves RIT students by maximizing clarity and minimizing visual debt, allows for high-velocity shipping in a 5-day sprint, and honors the Rams philosophy by staying "as invisible as possible."
